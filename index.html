About Us

It is the third largest state of India in terms of population and twelfth in terms of area . Dr. Shri Prakash Baranwal, President of Prabuddha Society Nechua Jalalpur, says that BIHAR has all the five names of India. In 2000 AD, a new state Jharkhand was created by separating the southern part of Bihar. Nepal is situated in the north of Bihar, Jharkhand in the south , West Bengal in the east , and Uttar Pradesh in the west . This region is situated in the fertile plains of the river Ganga and its tributaries. Ganga flows from west to east in it. Bihar is one of the greatest states of India. Bihar is also rich in natural beauty. The major rivers here include Ganga, Son, and Ghaghra. The major mountain ranges of Bihar include the foothills of the Himalayas. Bihar is an agricultural state. The major crops here include paddy, wheat, maize, and peas. Bihar also has many mineral deposits, such as coal, iron ore, and limestone. Bihar also has many industries. The major industries here include agriculture industry, textile industry, and mineral industry. Bihar also has many educational institutions, including Indian Institute of Technology (Patna), Patna University , and Nalanda University .

History

Present-day Bihar is made up of various historical regions. Regions of Bihar such as Magadha , Mithila and Anga are mentioned in religious texts and epics of ancient India. Magadha, in the region of modern-day western Bihar, became the centre of power, education and culture in India for 1000 years. In the Rigvedic period it was ruled by the Brihadratha dynasty. The Haryanka dynasty, founded in 684 BCE, ruled Magadha from the city of Rajagriha (modern Rajgir). Two famous kings of this dynasty were Bimbisara and his son Ajatashatru, who imprisoned his father to ascend the throne. Ajatashatru founded the city of Pataliputra which later became the capital of Magadha. He declared war and won the battle. The Haryanka dynasty was followed by the Shishunaga dynasty. Later the Nanda dynasty ruled over a vast empire extending from Bengal to Punjab. Buddhism in Magadha fell into decline due to the invasion of Muhammad bin Bakhtiyar Khilji, during which many of Bihar's world-famous universities of Nalanda and Vikramashila were destroyed. It was claimed that thousands of Buddhist monks were killed during the 12th century. D.N. Jha suggests, instead, that these incidents were the result of Buddhist Brahmin clashes in the struggle for supremacy. In 1540, the chieftain of the great Pashtuns, Sher Shah Suri of Sasaram , defeated Humayun 's Mughal army and took northern India from the Mughals. Sher Shah declared Delhi his capital and from the 11th century to the 20th century, Mithila was ruled by various indigenous dynasties. The first of these was the Karnat dynasty, followed by the Anwar dynasty, the Raghuvanshis and finally the Raj Darbhanga. During this period the capital of Mithila was shifted to Darbhanga.

Geography

Bihar is a Hindi speaking state in North India between 24°20'10" ~ 27°31'15" north latitude and 83°19'50" ~ 88°17'40" east longitude. The total area of ​​the state is 94,163 square kilometers, of which 92,257.51 square kilometers is rural area. After the separation of Jharkhand, the land of Bihar is mainly river plains and agricultural flat land. Situated in the eastern plains of the Ganges, the average height of this state is 173 feet. Geographically, Bihar is divided into three natural divisions - the mountainous and Terai region of the north, the vast plains of the center and the hilly edge of the south . The mountainous region of the north is a part of the Someshwar range. The average height of this range is 455 meters but its highest peak is 874 meters high. To the south of the Someshwar range is the Terai region. This is a marshy area where there are dense forests of Sal trees. The only tiger reserve of the state is located in Valmikinagar in these forests.

Language and Festivals

Hindi is the official language of Bihar and Urdu is the second official language. Maithili is the only Bihari language included in the Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution. Bhojpuri , Magahi , Angika and Bajjika are the other major languages ​​and dialects spoken in Bihar. The major festivals are Chhath , Holi , Diwali , Dussehra , Mahashivratri , Nag Panchami , Shri Panchami, Muharram , Eid and Christmas . Chhathi Maiya, the sixth form of Prakriti and Surya's sister, is worshipped during the festival. It is celebrated six days after Deepavali, or Tihar, on the sixth day of the lunar month of Kartika (October or November) in the Hindu calendar (Vikram Samvat), and hence why it is called Surya Shashti Vrata.[14][15][16] The rituals are observed over three nights and four days. They include holy bathing, fasting, and abstaining from drinking water (as a vrata), standing in water, and offering prasada (prayer offerings) and arghya to the rising and setting of the Sun. Some devotees also perform a prostration march as they head towards the riverbanks. All devotees prepare similar prasada (religious food) and offerings. Being the birthplace of the tenth Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji , Patna City (Patna) also witnesses heavy devotion on his birth anniversary.
